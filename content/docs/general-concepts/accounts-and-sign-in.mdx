A single **Sawala Cloud account** is your identity across the whole platform. You sign in once and reach every product, organization, and project you belong to.

## Create an account

1. Go to [sawala.cloud](https://sawala.cloud) and choose **Sign up**.
2. Register with your email or a supported social login.
3. Verify your email if prompted.

When your account is first created, Sawala Cloud sets up an **organization** for you automatically, so you have a workspace to start in right away. See [Organizations & projects](/id/docs/general-concepts/organizations-and-projects).

## Sign in

Return to [sawala.cloud](https://sawala.cloud) and choose **Sign in**. After signing in you land on the dashboard.

## The dashboard

The main dashboard lives at **[sawala.cloud/dashboard](https://sawala.cloud/dashboard)**. It's the home base for most of what you do without code:

- Switch between your organizations and projects.
- Manage content (Kontena), files (Berkasna), and forms (Formulir).
- Invite teammates and manage their roles.
- Mint and revoke [API keys](/id/docs/general-concepts/api-keys-and-access).
- Review usage and billing.

Some products have their own dashboard on a dedicated subdomain — for example **`kodena.sawala.cloud`** (hosting & deployment) and **`ajena.sawala.cloud`** (AI search).

<Callout type="info">
**One sign-in across the platform.** Your session is shared across all `*.sawala.cloud` subdomains, so once you're signed in at `sawala.cloud/dashboard` you can move to `kodena.sawala.cloud` or `ajena.sawala.cloud` without logging in again.
</Callout>

<Callout>
A few products also serve public, embeddable assets from their own domains — for example, forms are embedded from `formulir.id`. Those are delivery endpoints for your visitors, not places you sign in.
</Callout>

## Teams and roles

You don't need a personal account per workspace. Invite teammates into your **organization** and they sign in with their own Sawala Cloud account. Roles control who can edit content, manage members, or change billing — see [Organizations & projects](/id/docs/general-concepts/organizations-and-projects).
