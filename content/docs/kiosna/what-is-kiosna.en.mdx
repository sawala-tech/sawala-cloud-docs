**Kiosna** is Sawala Cloud's **B2B billing backbone**. It's how you bill business clients: keep a catalog of products and priced variants, sign clients to fixed-term **contracts**, generate **invoices**, record **payments** against them, and renew contracts with reminders. It's run from the dashboard by whoever handles your billing.

## When to choose Kiosna

Reach for Kiosna when you sell to other businesses on contracts and invoices, rather than self-serve checkout:

- Recurring service contracts with line items and renewals.
- Invoicing clients and tracking what's paid, partially paid, or outstanding.
- A billing record that keeps historical invoices correct even as your prices change.

## The billing chain

Each stage builds on the previous one, and prices are snapshotted forward so past documents stay accurate:

- **Products & SKUs** — your catalog. A product is a commercial family; a SKU is a priced, sellable variant (one-time or recurring).
- **Clients** — the businesses you invoice, including the billing email where reminders go.
- **Contracts** — fixed-term deals with line items that snapshot SKU prices. A contract moves through `draft → active → ending soon → ended` (or renewed/cancelled).
- **Invoices** — generated from a contract or standalone. An invoice flows `draft → sent → partially paid → paid` (or void), with the total derived from subtotal, discount, and tax.
- **Payments** — recorded against an invoice. Multiple payments add up, so partial payments work naturally.
- **Renewals** — a renewed contract becomes a new contract linked to the previous one, with reminders as the end date approaches.

<Callout type="info">
Payments are **recorded manually** today — an operator logs a bank transfer with a reference note. Financial actions like issuing invoices and recording payments are limited to billing administrators.
</Callout>

Kiosna is managed from the **dashboard**. Your Sawala sign-in carries over — see [Accounts & sign-in](/en/docs/general-concepts/accounts-and-sign-in).
